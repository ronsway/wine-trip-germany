The Mosel: A Week of Slate, Riesling and a Winding River

A one-week route along the river, from Cochem and its castles in the north to Roman Trier in the south: five of Germany's top wineries, Europe's steepest vineyards, fairytale castles, and a grand finale dinner on the second-to-last evening at a three-Michelin-star restaurant.

Day-by-Day Itinerary

Trip dates: Thursday, Apr 29 to Thursday, May 6, 2027 (8 days). Evening arrival, evening departure on day 8. May is the ideal month for the Mosel: green, blooming, quiet and with comfortable prices. This is the star-route version: a single base in Bernkastel-Kues for all 7 nights, with no mid-trip hotel change. Each day is a trip out and back to the same place. Most wineries and sights are within 30 min of base; the north (HL in Winningen, Burg Eltz, Cochem) is grouped into one long day. The Michelin dinner falls on the second-to-last evening, 20 min from base. Direct El Al flights round trip to Frankfurt (details under Practical Details). A rental car is essential.

DAY 1 · THU, APR 29, 2027

Arrival and Drive to Bernkastel-Kues

LY355: TLV 15:00 → FRA 18:35 → Bernkastel-Kues (overnight: Ferienhaus Mosel-Herberge · 7 nights) 📍 Map
  • Land in Frankfurt at 18:35, pick up the car and drive to Bernkastel-Kues (~1.5 to 2 hours). Arrival around 21:00-21:30
  • Check in to your single base for the whole week, a short evening in the old town or on the promenade
  • First glass of Riesling with Flammkuchen at a local Weinstube, if still open
Tip: Unpack once for the entire trip. The flight lands late, so today is just arrival and sleep — all the action starts tomorrow.
DAY 2 · FRI, APR 30, 2027

The Northern Day: Burg Eltz, Winningen and Cochem

Bernkastel → Burg Eltz → Winningen → Cochem → Bernkastel (overnight: Bernkastel-Kues) 📍 Map
  • Early start heading north: drive to Burg Eltz (~1 hour from Bernkastel), Germany's most beautiful castle, hidden in a forest. A lovely 40-minute walk from the Ringelsteiner Mühle car park. Arrive at opening time, before the crowds
  • Midday: drive to Winningen (~25 minutes from Burg Eltz) for a tasting at Heymann-Löwenstein (~13:00), the king of the Lower Mosel terraces and stony, dry Riesling
  • Afternoon: on the way back, stop in Cochem — Reichsburg Castle above the town and the chairlift to the Pinnerkreuz viewpoint, the finest angle over the town, castle and river
  • Evening: back to Bernkastel and dinner in the old town
Tip: This is the longest driving day (the far northern end of the valley, ~70 min each way). Leave early and you'll sweep up the whole north — HL, Burg Eltz and Cochem — in one go, freeing the rest of the week.
DAY 3 · SAT, MAY 1, 2027

An Easy Day at Base (Holiday) + Bernkastel

Bernkastel-Kues (option: cruise/drive to Beilstein) 📍 Map
  • Relaxed morning in Bernkastel: the old town, the market square with its timber-framed houses, and a climb up to the famous Doctor vineyard for a view over the river
  • The Vinothek at the St. Nikolaus-Hospital monastery — over 150 Mosel wines to taste in one place, a perfect opening to the wine week
  • Optional: a midday cruise or short drive to Beilstein, the "Pearl of the Mosel", a tiny baroque village with the Metternich castle ruins
  • Evening: a relaxed dinner in the old town, a lighter day in the middle of the trip
Note: May 1 is a public holiday in Germany (Labour Day) — some sights and shops are closed, so an easy day at base fits perfectly, especially after the long northern day. The Heymann-Löwenstein tasting has moved to Apr 30 (the winery is closed on the holiday).
DAY 4 · SUN, MAY 2, 2027

The Calmont and Traben-Trarbach: The Challenge Day

Bernkastel → Bremm (Calmont) → Traben-Trarbach → Bernkastel (overnight: Bernkastel-Kues) 📍 Map
  • Calmont Klettersteig: the trail through Europe's steepest vineyard above Bremm, with ladders and cables. About 3 hours, with breathtaking views over the river's horseshoe bend
  • Easier version: drive up to the Calmont-Gipfelkreuz viewpoint or take the panorama trail from Bremm
  • On the way: stop at the Marienburg viewpoint above Pünderich, the spot where the river appears on both sides of one ridge
  • Afternoon in Traben-Trarbach: Art Nouveau architecture and a tour of the town's underground wine cellars
  • Evening: back to Bernkastel (~40 min from Bremm) and dinner in the old town
Gear: The Klettersteig requires good hiking shoes, water and a solid head for heights. Do not attempt it in the rain.
DAY 5 · MON, MAY 3, 2027

Bernkastel-Kues, Wine Capital of the Mosel

A day on foot in Bernkastel-Kues (overnight: Bernkastel-Kues) 📍 Map
  • The old town: the market square, painted half-timbered houses and the crooked Spitzhäuschen
  • Walk up to the Landshut castle ruins through the Bernkasteler Doctor vineyard, Germany's most expensive and famous vineyard
  • Midday: the Vinothek in the cellars of the St. Nikolaus-Hospital in Kues, a vaulted stone cellar with about 150 Mosel wines to taste freely
  • Afternoon: tasting at Dr. Loosen, at a beautiful estate on the riverbank
  • Evening: Doctor Weinstube or Rotisserie Royale in the old town's alleys
Tip: A completely car-free day. Everything is walkable, so feel free to go all in on the tastings.
DAY 6 · TUE, MAY 4, 2027

Wehlen and Zeltingen: The Big Winery Day

Bernkastel → Wehlen → Zeltingen (overnight: Bernkastel-Kues) 📍 Map
  • Morning: Joh. Jos. Prüm in Wehlen, a true cult winery. Visits by personal appointment only, and worth every effort
  • Walk the vineyard trail from Wehlen to Zeltingen along the Wehlener Sonnenuhr, the famous sundial among the vines (about 4 km), with a market picnic beside one of the vintners' huts in the vineyards
  • Extension for the energetic: continue toward Ürzig to see the Ürziger Würzgarten, the "spice garden", a rare red-slate vineyard that yields spicy, exotic Riesling
  • Afternoon: Markus Molitor, one of Germany's most impressive wineries, with dozens of single-vineyard wines
  • Evening: a light country dinner at a Gasthaus in Zeltingen, saving room for tomorrow
Tip: If JJ Prüm isn't available, an excellent alternative in the same village is S.A. Prüm, with a proper visitor center and tastings without complicated booking.
DAY 7 · WED, MAY 5, 2027 · THE BIG NIGHT

Piesport, the Last Winery and the Michelin Dinner

Bernkastel → Neumagen-Dhron → Brauneberg → Piesport → Bernkastel (overnight: Bernkastel-Kues) 📍 Map
  • Relaxed morning: a scenic drive to Neumagen-Dhron, "Germany's oldest wine village". The special experience: a short sail on the Stella Noviomagi, a reconstructed Roman wine ship, glass of Riesling in hand (seasonal, book ahead)
  • Midday: tasting at Fritz Haag in Brauneberg, masters of the Juffer Sonnenuhr (by appointment)
  • Afternoon: viewpoint over the Piesporter Goldtröpfchen, the famous amphitheater of vines, and a rest before the evening
  • Evening: schanz.restaurant in Piesport, three Michelin stars. The trip's grand finale, just 15 minutes from Bernkastel
Very important: schanz is closed Sunday through Tuesday (confirmed). Our Michelin evening falls on Wednesday (May 5), the first day they open each week — perfect. Reserve two to three months ahead. The most convenient option: sleep at the restaurant's own small hotel and skip driving after wine entirely.
DAY 8 · THU, MAY 6, 2027

Roman Trier and Departure

Bernkastel → Trier → Frankfurt (LY356: FRA 19:45 → TLV 00:50) 📍 Map
  • Check out and drive to Trier, Germany's oldest city (about 50 minutes). A Wednesday — everything is open and crowd-free
  • Porta Nigra, the cathedral, the market square and the Roman amphitheater (UNESCO World Heritage sites)
  • One last lunch at a Weinstube facing the cathedral
  • Drive back to Frankfurt (about 2.5 hours), return the car and catch the direct El Al evening flight home
Why return via Frankfurt: There are no direct flights from Luxembourg to Tel Aviv, and El Al flies direct only from Frankfurt. Returning the car in the same country also saves a 100-200 euro drop-off fee. Leave Trier by 2:00 PM for an evening flight. Wednesday advantage: Trier is much quieter than on a weekend.

The Grand Dinner · Day 6

★ ★ ★

schanz. restaurant

Piesport · Three Michelin stars · Chef Thomas Schanz · The second-to-last evening

A local son who trained under Germany's greatest chefs (Harald Wohlfahrt, Klaus Erfort) and returned to the small family business in Piesport, in the heart of the Goldtröpfchen vineyard. Astonishingly precise modern French cuisine, an exceptional Mosel wine list, and surprisingly fair prices for this level. Full tasting menu: around 250 to 300 euros per person before wine. The location is perfect for the route: fifteen minutes from your Bernkastel base, and you can also stay at the restaurant's own hotel.

Alternatives if fully booked: Waldhotel Sonnora in Dreis (three stars, about 25 minutes from Bernkastel), or Rüssel's Landhaus near Trittenheim (one star, delightful country atmosphere).

A single base for the whole week in the heart of Bernkastel-Kues. Unpack once, and return to the same place every evening — almost equidistant to all the wineries and sights.

Ferienhaus Mosel-Herberge

Bernkastel · old town · your base for all 7 nights (Apr 29 – May 6)

Food and Wine Along the Way

Beyond the Michelin evening, the Mosel is a paradise of simple, excellent country food that pairs perfectly with Riesling.

Flammkuchen

At every Weinstube

A paper-thin "pizza" with cream, onion and bacon. The national dish of the wine regions, wonderful with dry Riesling.

Winzerteller (Vintner's Plate)

Wine cellars

A board of cheeses, cured meats, country bread and pickles. The classic companion to tastings.

Riesling Crème Brûlée and Plums

Bernkastel and Cochem

Wine-based desserts and seasonal plum cake (Zwetschgenkuchen). With an afternoon coffee.

Federweisser

If you visit in autumn

Fizzy, half-fermented young wine sold only during harvest season, served with onion tart. A true local seasonal experience.

Trier's Market and Delis

Hauptmarkt, Trier

A daily market with cheese, bread and fruit stalls. Perfect for the last day's lunch.

Red Vineyard Peach (Weinbergpfirsich)

Throughout the region

A wild red peach that grows only among the Mosel vineyards. Its liqueur, jam and desserts are the most local culinary souvenir there is. The liqueur with Sekt is a must.

Local Sekt

Cochem and Winningen

German sparkling wine made from Riesling by the traditional method. Small producers along the river make excellent versions at wonderful prices.

The Trip in (Amusing) Numbers

A few figures to get you excited, and to supply conversation material with the first glass of Riesling.

~650
km of driving

For the entire week. With 16 stops, 5 wineries and zero traffic jams along the way.

65°
Calmont's slope

Europe's steepest vineyard. For comparison, a mall escalator runs at 30 degrees. The vintners there work with harnesses.

~50
wines tasted

Five wineries plus the Vinothek with 150 open bottles. At a reasonable pace you'll taste more wines in a week than most people do in a year.

2,000+
years of wine

The Romans planted vines here in the first century AD. You'll be drinking the upgraded version of what the legionnaires drank.

×2.7
crooked river

The Mosel meanders so much it covers 243 km in Germany over a straight-line distance of about 90 km. You will always arrive before it does.

~800
meters of climbing

Calmont, Landshut castle, Reichsburg and Burg Eltz combined. Roughly two and a half Empire State Buildings, with wine at the end.

3:2,000
stars per residents

Tiny Piesport holds 3 Michelin stars for about 2,000 residents. A stars-per-capita ratio that embarrasses Paris.

100+
year-old vines

The steep slate slopes still hold century-old ungrafted vines, among Europe's oldest. Phylloxera doesn't like slate.

Best Season

  • May-June: green, blooming and calm
  • September-October: harvest, wine festivals and Federweisser, the most festive time (and the busiest)
  • Two festivals worth aiming for: the Winningen wine festival (late August, Germany's oldest) and Bernkastel's great wine festival (early September, with fireworks over the river)
